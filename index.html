    ABOUT


    Dima Hasao, formerly known as North Cachar Hills, is a district located in the state of Assam, India. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and diverse ethnic communities. Here's a brief overview of the history of Dima Hasao:
    Early History: The region of Dima Hasao has a long history that dates back to ancient times. It was inhabited by various indigenous tribes, each with its own distinct culture and traditions. These tribes include the Dimasa, Zeme Naga, Hmar, Kuki, and Hrangkhol, among others.

    Colonial Era: During the British colonial period, the region was known as the North Cachar Hills. The British established administrative control over the area and integrated it into the Assam province. The region remained relatively isolated due to its rugged terrain and limited infrastructure. Formation of Dima Hasao District: In 1970, the North Cachar Hills Autonomous District Council (NCHADC) was established to give the local tribes a degree of self-governance. On 1st February 1972, the district's name was officially changed to Dima Hasao, reflecting the predominant Dimasa tribe's presence.

    Ethnic Struggles: The region witnessed ethnic tensions and conflicts among the different tribal communities, often centered around issues related to land, identity, and autonomy. These conflicts have, at times, resulted in violence and displacement.

    Autonomy and Development: Over the years, efforts have been made to promote the overall development of the district. The Dima Hasao Autonomous Council (DHAC) was established to provide more local autonomy and facilitate self-governance for the various tribes. The council works on issues related to governance, infrastructure development, education, and healthcare. Tourism and Natural Beauty: Dima Hasao is renowned for its breathtaking natural beauty, characterized by lush green landscapes, scenic hills, rivers, and waterfalls. The district offers opportunities for eco-tourism, trekking, and exploring the rich flora and fauna of the region.

    Cultural Heritage: The diverse ethnic communities of Dima Hasao have preserved their unique cultures and traditions. Each tribe has its own distinct language, attire, festivals, and customs. This cultural diversity contributes to the vibrant tapestry of the region.

    Challenges: Despite its natural beauty and cultural richness, Dima Hasao has faced challenges such as underdevelopment, inadequate infrastructure, and periodic ethnic tensions. Efforts are ongoing to address these issues and promote sustainable development. Overall, Dima Hasao, now known as "The Switzerland of the East," continues to evolve while cherishing its history, cultural diversity, and the aspiration to achieve greater socio-economic progress.

    Dima Hasao, with its stunning natural landscapes, has several beautiful places that are worth visiting. Here are some of the most beautiful places in Dima Hasao, Assam:

    Haflong : The district headquarters and the largest town of Dima Hasao, Haflong is known for its picturesque beauty. Surrounded by rolling hills, lush greenery, and serene lakes, Haflong offers breathtaking views. The Haflong Lake and the Borail Range are some of the attractions here.

    Jatinga : Famous for the mysterious "bird suicides," Jatinga is a unique phenomenon where birds are said to descend during certain months. The village is surrounded by lush forests and is an interesting spot for bird enthusiasts.

    Maibong : Maibong is known for its historical significance and scenic beauty. It was the ancient capital of the Dimasa Kingdom. The ruins of the Kachari Kingdom's palace, stone temples, and archaeological sites add to its charm.

    Umrangso : This region is known for its natural beauty and abundant water resources. The Kopili River, Umrangso Reservoir, and the nearby hot water springs are major attractions. Umrangso is also home to hydropower projects.

    Panimoor : It's a popular trekking destination surrounded by lush forests and picturesque landscapes. The Panimoor Waterfall is a notable attraction, cascading down from a considerable height.

    Maibang Hot Water Spring : Located in the Maibang town area, this hot water spring is believed to have therapeutic properties. It's a serene spot surrounded by greenery.

    Khobak: A scenic village known for its terraced fields, Khobak offers panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys. The trek to Khobak from Haflong is quite rewarding.

    Umrongso Reservoir : Also known as the Umrangso Dam, this reservoir is surrounded by hills and offers a tranquil atmosphere. It's a great place for boating and enjoying the natural surroundings.

    Panimoor Waterfall: This picturesque waterfall is located near Haflong and requires a trek through the dense forest. The journey and the waterfall itself are worth the effort.

    Sutung Waterfall: Located near Haflong, Sutung Waterfall is a hidden gem. The waterfall cascades down from a height and is surrounded by lush greenery.
    These are just a few of the many beautiful places that Dima Hasao has to offer. The district's natural beauty, coupled with its rich cultural diversity, makes it a fascinating destination for travelers looking to explore the hidden gems of northeastern India.

    Dima Hasao is a predominantly rural district with an economy largely based on agriculture, forestry, and small-scale industries. The district's economy is also influenced by its natural resources, including tea plantations, timber, and minerals. The income of Dima Hasao District would include various sources:

    Agriculture : Agriculture is a significant contributor to the district's economy. The region cultivates crops like rice, maize, ginger, turmeric, and various fruits. Tea cultivation is also an important agricultural activity.

    Forestry : The district's forest resources play a role in generating income through timber, bamboo, and other non-timber forest products.

    Small-Scale Industries : There are small-scale industries in the district, including handicrafts, handlooms, and traditional crafts. These industries provide employment opportunities and contribute to the local economy.

    Tourism : The natural beauty of Dima Hasao, with its scenic landscapes and cultural diversity, attracts some tourism. Tourism-related activities, such as hospitality services and local crafts, can contribute to the local economy.

    Government Services : As with any district, government employment and services also contribute to the income of the area.

    Remittances : Like many regions in India, remittances from people working outside the district or country can also contribute to the local income.

    It's important to note that the economic situation of a region can change due to various factors, including policy changes, economic development initiatives, and external influences.
